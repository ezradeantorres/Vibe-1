# Hidden Gem Editor Handbook

Welcome, Elena. This guide walks you through updating the words and photos on your website (`hiddengemhealingutah.com`) using the editor built right into the live site. No special dashboard, no extra app — you just visit your own website, sign in, and click into anything you want to change.

If you've used Google Docs or Squarespace, you already have enough background. There's nothing technical to learn.

---

## 1. What this editor lets you do

Your website has a small, hidden editor wired directly into every page. Once you sign in, you can change text and swap photos by clicking on them — the same way you'd click into a paragraph in Google Docs.

**You CAN edit:**

- Headings (the big page titles)
- Paragraphs of body text
- Bullet-list items
- The text on buttons and call-to-action banners (like "Book a consultation")
- Photos — you can swap any photo for one you upload from your computer
- Quotes (like the testimonials on the home page)
- Footer text

**You CAN'T edit (yet) with this tool:**

- The structure of pages — adding or removing whole sections, or rearranging them
- The colors, fonts, or overall layout
- The navigation menu (the links at the top of every page)
- The fields on the booking form
- Adding a brand-new page

For any of those, send a note to Ezra and he'll make the change for you.

## 2. How to sign in

1. Open your website in any browser. You can be on any page — home, about, Abbey's page, either of Sara's pages — it doesn't matter.
2. Scroll all the way to the bottom of the page. In the footer, look for a small word that says **edit**. It sits next to the "Privacy Practices" and "No Surprises Act" links, separated by a small dot.
3. Click **edit**. A sign-in box pops up in the middle of your screen, titled "Editor sign-in."
4. Type the editor password into the box. (Ezra will have shared this with you. If you don't have it, just ask him.)
5. Click **Sign in**. If the password is correct, the sign-in box closes and a dark bar appears at the top of the page that says "Editing as Editor — click any text to change it." That bar is your signal that you're in edit mode.

If you typed the password wrong, the box will say "Incorrect password." in red. Try again. Nothing bad happens from getting it wrong a few times.

**A few things to know about staying signed in:**

- Signing in works only in the browser tab you signed in on. If you close that tab, you'll need to sign in again next time.
- Signing in on your phone does not sign you in on your laptop. They're separate.
- After a while of being signed in, the editor will quietly expire your session. If you click something and nothing happens, or if a message pops up saying "Your editor sign-in has expired," just click **edit** in the footer again and re-enter the password.

## 3. How to edit text

Once you're signed in and you see the "Editing as Editor" bar at the top:

1. Click directly on the heading, paragraph, bullet item, or button text you want to change. The text becomes editable, exactly like clicking into a Google Doc.
2. Type your new text. You can also select-and-delete, paste, or use your arrow keys, just like in any text box.
3. When you're done with that piece of text, click somewhere else on the page (or just click into the next piece of text you want to edit). Your change is held in place, but it isn't saved yet.
4. When you've finished all your edits for this visit, click the **Save** button on the right side of the dark bar at the top of the page. It will say "Saving…" for a moment, then return to normal — and the edit bar will close, meaning you've left edit mode and your changes are saved.

If you change your mind in the middle of editing and want to throw away everything you've done in this session, click **Cancel** instead of Save. The page will snap back to how it was when you started.

**About when your changes show up publicly:**

Your edits save instantly to your website's storage. But the live public site is "baked" — meaning re-built into final static pages — once an hour, on a schedule. So:

- In the editor, your changes appear right away when you reload the page.
- On the public website (what your visitors see), your changes can take **up to one hour** to appear.

This is normal. If something hasn't shown up after a full hour, then it's worth telling Ezra.

**One small extra feature:** When you click into a piece of text in edit mode, a little button labeled **"Reset this field"** appears just above it. Clicking that throws away your edits to just that one piece of text and brings back the original wording. It's there as a safety net — feel free to use it if you've made a mess of one paragraph and want to start over.

## 4. The "what about pasting from Word?" warning

This is the single most common way to accidentally mess up the look of your site, so please read this one carefully.

**Don't paste rich text directly from Word, Google Docs, Apple Mail, or any similar program** into the editor. When you copy from those programs, your computer secretly carries along all of the original formatting — fonts, colors, spacing, weird hidden tags. When you paste it onto your website, that hidden formatting comes with it and clashes with the site's own design. The result is usually text that looks the wrong size, wrong color, or has odd backgrounds behind it.

Two safe ways to paste:

- **Paste without formatting.** Most browsers have this built in. On a Mac, press `Cmd+Shift+V` instead of the usual `Cmd+V`. On Windows, press `Ctrl+Shift+V` instead of `Ctrl+V`. This strips out the formatting and pastes just the plain words.
- **Paste through a plain-text app first.** On a Mac, open TextEdit and switch it to plain-text mode (Format → Make Plain Text). On Windows, open Notepad. Paste your text in there first, then copy from there into your website editor. By the time the words reach the site, all the hidden formatting has been scrubbed off.

If you ever paste something and it suddenly looks wrong — different font, weird highlight color, extra spacing — click the **"Reset this field"** button (described above) to bring back the original, and try again with one of the safer paste methods.

## 5. How to swap a photo

1. Make sure you're in edit mode (see section 2).
2. Hover over the photo you want to replace — your cursor will turn into a pointer, and a tooltip will say "Click to replace this image."
3. Click the photo. A regular file-picker window opens, the same one you use when attaching a file to an email.
4. Find the new photo on your computer and click "Open" (or "Choose").
5. The photo briefly looks faded while it uploads, then snaps to the new image. That's it — no extra Save click is needed for photo swaps; the upload itself saves it.
6. As with text edits, the new photo appears immediately in your editor view, but can take up to one hour to appear on the public site.

**Tips for choosing photos:**

- For the big "hero" photo at the top of each page, use a wide (landscape) photo. Tall (portrait) photos get awkwardly cropped in those spots.
- For team or sidebar photos, portrait orientation is fine.
- Try to keep each photo file under about 2 MB. Larger files make your website slow to load, especially on phones. If your photo is bigger than that, almost any photo app (Mac Photos, Windows Photos, even an online tool like squoosh.app) can save a smaller copy.
- iPhone photos are fine — just AirDrop them to your laptop, or email them to yourself, before you upload.
- **Please don't upload photos that already have words written on them.** The website automatically adds its own headings and buttons on top of photos, and you'll end up with overlapping text that's hard to read. Use clean photos and let the site's own text sit on top.

## 6. How to replace a placeholder testimonial with a real one

Your home page currently shows three placeholder testimonials. Here's how to put a real quote in place of one:

1. Sign in (section 2).
2. Scroll down the home page to the testimonials section.
3. Click into the quote itself — the words inside the quotation marks. Select the placeholder text and type the new quote in its place.
4. Click into the name line beneath the quote. Replace the placeholder name with the real person's name.
5. If there's a line under the name (a city, or a phrase like "Patient since 2023"), click into that and replace it too. If you don't want a label there, you can delete it and leave the line blank.
6. Click **Save** in the bar at the top.
7. Give it up to an hour to appear on the public site.

You can do this for as many of the placeholder cards as the section has slots for. If you'd like a fourth (or fifth) testimonial card added to the section, that's a small structural change — send Ezra a note and he'll add it.

## 7. When something goes wrong

A short list of the things most likely to confuse you, and what to do about each:

- **"I edited something and saved, but it's not showing up on the live site."** Give it a full hour. The site re-builds itself on a schedule, once per hour. If it's been more than an hour and the change still isn't there, send Ezra a note with the page and the specific text you changed.

- **"I clicked 'edit' in the footer and nothing happens"** or **"I tried to click into a paragraph and it didn't become editable."** Your sign-in probably expired. Refresh the page (Cmd+R on Mac, Ctrl+R on Windows), then click **edit** in the footer and sign in again with the password.

- **"A message popped up saying my editor sign-in expired."** That's normal — sign-ins don't last forever. Click **edit** in the footer and sign in again. Anything you'd already saved before the message is safe; only unsaved changes from your last few minutes are lost.

- **"I deleted something by mistake and clicked Save."** Don't panic, and don't make any more edits to that page yet. Old versions of your text are recoverable, but it's much easier to restore them if you haven't kept editing on top. Send Ezra a note describing what you deleted and which page it was on.

- **"I uploaded a photo and it looks sideways or stretched."** Some photos (especially from phones) are stored with an invisible "please rotate this when displaying" flag that not every website handles. Rotate the photo on your computer first (in Photos, Preview, or any image tool), save it as a new file, and upload that new file in place of the bad one.

- **"The page looks broken — text in the wrong place, weird highlights, things overlapping."** First, refresh the page (Cmd+R or Ctrl+R). If it still looks wrong, you may be looking at a stuck edit-mode state in your browser — close the tab completely and reopen the site in a new one. If it's still broken after that, take a screenshot of what you see and send it to Ezra.

- **"I see a yellow banner that says someone else is editing."** That just means another authorized person (probably Ezra) is in the editor right now. The site is built so only one person can make changes at a time, to avoid two people overwriting each other. Wait a few minutes and the banner will clear when the other person saves or cancels.

- **"I want to wipe everything I've done on a page and start fresh."** When you're signed in, there's a **Reset page** button in the dark bar at the top, next to Save and Cancel. It will ask you to confirm, then erase every edit you've ever saved on that one page and bring back the original wording and photos. Use this carefully — it can't be undone from inside the editor. If you only want to reset one paragraph, use the smaller "Reset this field" button that pops up when you click into a piece of text instead.
