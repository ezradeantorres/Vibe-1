About Sunflower Ridge Assisted Living

A different kind of home — on purpose.

Most assisted living communities are built around scale: dozens or hundreds of residents, long hallways, name tags, and rotating staff. We've built around something else.

We bundle daily life into one straightforward monthly fee — meals, housekeeping, activities, care, and the things you don't think about. We meet residents where they are today and adapt as life changes.

Sunflower Ridge is one house with eight residents, run by a family that knows everyone by name and lives like the help is personal. Because it is.

Our approach

Three things we do differently.

We're not optimizing for scale. We're optimizing for the experience of one resident, on one ordinary Tuesday.

01

Eight residents — by design.

We could fit more. We don't. A small home means caregivers know what every resident eats for breakfast, sleeps with, worries about. The math of attention only works at this size.

02

Family-style, not facility-style.

Meals are served at one table. Activities are picked by the people doing them. The dog isn't a 'therapy animal' — it just lives here. The whole place is built like a home, because it is one.

03

Care plans that change as life does.

Aging is rarely a straight line. We review and adjust each resident's care plan as needs shift — without renegotiating contracts, raising the rate, or moving them to a new wing.

Common questions

Questions families ask.

What's actually included in the monthly rate?

Room, three meals a day plus snacks, all care (bathing, dressing, medication, mobility), the beautician, the dental visit, activities, devotionals, the dog. We don't tier you by 'level of care.' One rate, no upcharges.

What if my parent's needs change?

We sit down with the family, talk it through, and update the care plan. Most changes happen gradually, and we work to keep transitions as gentle as possible — same room, same staff, just a little more support. We don't move people to a new wing or raise the rate when needs shift.

Can residents bring their own furniture?

Yes — and we strongly encourage it. The familiar chair, the photographs, the bedside lamp from home — these matter. We'll help measure the room ahead of move-in so you can plan what fits.

Is someone awake at night?

Yes. There is always at least one caregiver awake and on shift — not just on call — overnight, every night. That's non-negotiable.

Can residents come and go as they please?

Yes. Family visits, outings, holidays, appointments — we just like to know so we can coordinate care and meals. Assisted living means support, not loss of independence.

Do you allow pets?

Visiting pets are welcome. Our resident dog is hard not to fall for. For move-in pets, we look at it case by case — small, settled animals are usually fine.
